Population & Demographics

Population (2024): Estimated at 118,277,063 people. Ethnic Composition (2020, CIA World Factbook): Tagalog: 26% Bisaya/Binisaya: 14.3% Ilocano: 8% Cebuano: 8% Illonggo: 7.9% Bikol/Bicol: 6.5% Waray: 3.8% Other local ethnicities: 18.5% Foreign ethnicities: 0.2% Languages (2020, CIA World Factbook): Tagalog: Spoken by 39.9% Bisaya/Binisaya: Spoken by 16% Hiligaynon/Ilonggo: Spoken by 7.3% Ilocano: Spoken by 7.1% Cebuano: Spoken by 6.5% Official Languages: Filipino (based on Tagalog) and English Recognized Major Dialects: Eight major dialects Religion (2020, CIA World Factbook): Roman Catholic: 78.8% Muslim: 6.4% Iglesia ni Cristo: 2.6% Other Christian denominations and religious affiliations: 12.1% Cultural Influences: Catholicism: Rooted in the Spanish colonial period, it profoundly shapes societal norms and values. National Identity: Shaped by the struggle for independence but strongly tied to family, local communities, and provinces. Community Values: Collective interests and pride in family and traditions. Respectful interactions based on age and social status (e.g., terms like "kuya" and "ate"). Concept of "hiya" (saving face) emphasizing social etiquette and hospitality. Family Structures: Multi-generational households with a strong focus on respecting elders. Modern Influences: Shifting practices in dating and marriage with increasing individual choice.
